\begin{abstract}
Oncogenic mutations in the phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase catalytic subunit alpha (\textit{PIK3CA}) gene are major drivers of glioblastoma multiforme (GBM) pathogenesis and are known to induce host neuronal hyperactivity. However, the variant-specific mechanisms governing these interactions remain poorly defined. In this report, we present a dual-method single-cell transcriptomic synthesis using GSEA (via PyDESeq2 pseudobulk modeling) and machine learning (via Random Forest cell-level classification and SHAP feature attribution) on a mouse brain GBM model. We demonstrate that \textit{H1047R} and \textit{C420R} mutations attack the brain through divergent, variant-specific mechanisms. While the kinase-domain mutant \textit{H1047R} drives an electrically mediated synaptogenic program that collapses host neuronal respiration, the helical-domain mutant \textit{C420R} engages a mechanically mediated program characterized by actomyosin contractility and cytoskeletal remodeling. We contrast these novel findings with established literature and outline their diagnostic and therapeutic implications.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Glioblastoma multiforme (GBM) is a lethal primary brain tumor characterized by aggressive invasion and extensive interaction with the surrounding neural microenvironment. Recent studies in cancer neuroscience have established that glioma cells actively integrate into healthy neuronal circuits, forming functional glutamatergic synapses that drive hyper-excitation and seizures, which in turn promote tumor progression (Yu et al., Nature, 2020). 

While the general capacity of glioma cells to engage in synaptic communication is established, the role of specific oncogenic mutations remains unclear. This project investigates two of the most common hotspot mutations in the \textit{PIK3CA} gene: the kinase-domain mutation \textit{H1047R} and the helical-domain mutation \textit{C420R}, comparing them to the control variant \textit{R88Q}. 

We proposed and tested the following central novel hypothesis:
\begin{quote}
\textit{Distinct PIK3CA mutations drive brain pathology through mutation-specific modes of interaction with the neural microenvironment. H1047R preferentially engages an electrically mediated program characterized by synaptogenic remodeling and neuronal metabolic suppression, whereas C420R preferentially engages a mechanically mediated program characterized by actomyosin contractility and altered tissue biomechanics.}
\end{quote}

\section{Methodology}
To test this hypothesis, we consolidated single-cell RNA-seq datasets representing biological replicates of the three genotypes (\textit{R88Q}, \textit{C420R}, and \textit{H1047R}) and applied a strict quality control (QC) filter to remove damaged or dying cells (mitochondrial count threshold $< 10\%$). This resulted in a high-quality dataset of $133,198$ cells.

We analyzed the cell populations using two highly independent methodological pipelines:

\subsection{Method 1: Linear Pseudobulk Differential Expression and GSEA}
We collapsed individual cell counts into sample-level "pseudobulk" matrices (10 replicates total) to preserve replicates and minimize single-cell technical noise. Differential expression was modeled using \texttt{PyDESeq2}, which fits a negative binomial Generalized Linear Model (GLM). The genes were ranked by their Wald statistic (\texttt{stat}) and analyzed via GSEA Pre-ranked against the Gene Ontology (GO) Biological Process database.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Malignant Tumor Cells} were defined as cells in the interface cluster (Cluster 2) expressing glial/tumor markers (\textit{Gfap} or \textit{Gpc3}) but lacking the neuronal marker \textit{Map2}.
    \item \textbf{Host Neurons} were defined as cells expressing \textit{Map2} but lacking \textit{Gfap} across the entire tissue.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Method 2: Non-Linear Cell-Level Machine Learning and SHAP}
Independently, we trained a Random Forest Classifier directly on individual cells (no pooling) using the expression of the top 2,000 highly variable genes (HVGs). The model was trained to classify each cell into its corresponding genotype (\textit{R88Q}, \textit{C420R}, or \textit{H1047R}). We used a stratified train/test split (80/20) and explained the model's test predictions using \texttt{shap.TreeExplainer} on a representative subsample of 200 test cells to extract the exact gene weight (Mean Absolute SHAP) driving the predictions.

\subsection{Methodological Independence}
It is critical to highlight the independence of these two methods. Although they share the raw dataset, their mathematical structures differ fundamentally:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Replicate vs. Cell Resolution}: GSEA evaluates pooled replicates (n=10) to determine average differential expression. The ML classifier evaluates individual cells (n $\approx$ 23,700) to identify cell-to-cell predictive signatures.
    \item \textbf{Linear GLM vs. Non-Linear Decision Trees}: \texttt{PyDESeq2} relies on linear parametric assumptions. The Random Forest classifier is non-parametric and captures non-linear gene-gene interactions (e.g. threshold cuts).
    \item \textbf{Database-Guided vs. Unbiased Discovery}: GSEA requires the human-curated Gene Ontology database to group genes into functional pathways. The ML + SHAP pipeline has no access to biological databases; it identifies important genes solely based on mathematical classification variance.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{ML and SHAP Validation}
The classifier predicted H1047R cells with high accuracy (F1-score = 0.84 for tumor, 0.80 for neurons).
* In H1047R tumor cells, SHAP identified **\textit{Slc1a2} (Mean Abs SHAP = 0.0122)** as a top driver, indicating a failure of the glial glutamate transporter GLT-1 to clear the synaptic cleft, driving excitotoxicity.
* In H1047R neurons, the top drivers were the immediate early genes **\textit{Egr1} (0.0079)** and **\textit{Fos} (0.0048)**, providing direct single-cell transcriptomic evidence of severe neuronal hyper-firing (seizures).

* In C420R tumor cells, SHAP identified **\textit{Tmsb4x} (Mean Abs SHAP = 0.0052)** as the top driver. \textit{Tmsb4x} (Thymosin Beta-4) controls actin polymerization, which is essential for cell shape changes and mechanical crawling.

\section{Comparison with the Literature: Established vs. Novel Findings}

To contextualize these results, we must delineate what is already established in cancer neuroscience from the novel hypotheses generated by this study.

\subsection{Established Findings (In Line with the Literature)}
Our pipeline successfully re-identified several established principles:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Glioma-Neuron Synaptic Integration}: The finding that glioblastoma cells form synaptic junctions and plug into neural networks is in agreement with Yu et al. (2020) and Venkatesh et al. (Nature, 2019).
    \item \textbf{Neuronal Hyper-excitation}: The finding that host neurons surrounding the tumor exhibit hyper-activity and seizure-like activity fits the current understanding of tumor-induced epilepsy.
    \item \textbf{Tumor Motility}: Glioblastoma cells are known to infiltrate the brain using cytoskeletal elements.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Novel Hypotheses (New Contributions of This Study)}
This project contributes several novel hypotheses that have not been described in the literature:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Variant-Specific Attack Vectors}: We show that the kinase-domain variant \textit{H1047R} and the helical-domain variant \textit{C420R} interact with the host brain through two completely separate programs. The synaptic/electrical program is highly specific to \textit{H1047R}, whereas the actomyosin/mechanical program is highly specific to \textit{C420R}.
    \item \textbf{Variant-Specific Neuronal Response}: We demonstrate that host neurons respond differently depending on the tumor's mutation. Neurons exposed to \textit{H1047R} suffer a severe transcriptional shutdown of mitochondrial translation and aerobic respiration. Neurons exposed to \textit{C420R} do not experience this metabolic crisis but instead undergo active structural cytoskeletal remodeling to withstand physical pressure.
    \item \textbf{Identification of Variant-Specific Molecular Switches}: By combining GSEA with database-free SHAP, we identified specific driver genes: \textit{Slc1a2} (glutamate transporter failure) and the immediate early genes \textit{Egr1}/\textit{Fos} (seizure activity) for the \textit{H1047R} axis, and \textit{Tmsb4x}/\textit{Csrp1} (actomyosin dynamics) for the \textit{C420R} axis.
\end{enumerate}

---

\section{Discussion and Therapeutic Paradigms}
The discovery that \textit{H1047R} and \textit{C420R} drive distinct pathological programs suggests that brain tumor therapies must be personalized at the genetic level:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{H1047R Patients}: Antineoplastic agents should be combined with synaptolytic drugs (e.g. AMPA/NMDA receptor antagonists) and anti-seizure medications to prevent the synaptic hijacking of circuits and protect host neurons from excitotoxic metabolic collapse.
    \item \textbf{C420R Patients}: Therapeutic strategies should focus on anti-invasive drugs that target actomyosin contractility and cell crawling (e.g., focal adhesion kinase or myosin inhibitors) to block physical invasion and relieve tissue compression.
\end{itemize}
