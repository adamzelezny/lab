\section{Introduction}
Mutations in the \textit{PIK3CA} gene in glioblastoma are known to trigger hyperactivity in host brain cells, but the physiological mechanisms remain an active area of research. By analyzing single-cell gene expression and machine learning models, we have formulated two distinct hypotheses explaining how the \textit{H1047R} and \textit{C420R} mutations may interact differently with the host brain.

---

\section{Hypothesis 1: The H1047R Mutation Enables Electrical Integration and Excitotoxic Exhaustion}

We hypothesize that the \textit{H1047R} mutation drives glioblastoma cells to establish direct, functional synaptic connections with neighboring neurons. By integration into these active neural circuits, the tumor cells may disrupt local signaling, forcing host neurons to fire excessively (leading to seizures). This continuous hyper-activation is hypothesized to cause a massive metabolic crisis, eventually starving the host neurons of ATP and shutting down their mitochondrial respiration.

\subsection*{Key Data Supporting the H1047R Synaptic Wiring Hypothesis (Malignant Cells vs. Control):}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Cell-Cell Adhesion via Plasma-Membrane Molecules} ($+2.31$ NES) --- Suggests active anchoring of the tumor cell membrane to the neuronal membrane.
    \item \textbf{Modulation of Chemical Synaptic Transmission} ($+2.29$ NES) --- Indicates upregulation of genes involved in neurotransmitter signaling.
    \item \textbf{Synapse Organization} ($+2.19$ NES) --- Suggests the tumor is constructing functional synaptic junctions.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Key Data Supporting the Neuronal Energy Exhaustion Hypothesis (Neurons - H1047R vs. C420R):}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Aerobic Respiration} ($-3.01$ NES) --- Indicates a severe downregulation in cell breathing.
    \item \textbf{Oxidative Phosphorylation} ($-2.97$ NES) --- Suggests the primary mitochondrial energy generator is shut down.
    \item \textbf{Mitochondrial ATP Synthesis} ($-2.82$ NES) --- Indicates the cells may be losing their ability to generate ATP.
    \item \textbf{Mitochondrial Translation} ($-2.61$ NES) --- Suggests mitochondrial protein building has stalled under stress.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Single-Cell Machine Learning \& SHAP Driver Gene Hypotheses:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{\textit{Slc1a2} (GLT-1) in Tumor Cells} --- The model's identification of the main glial glutamate transporter suggests that \textit{H1047R} glia may lose their ability to clear glutamate from the synaptic cleft, leaving excess glutamate to overstimulate neurons.
    \item \textbf{\textit{Egr1} \& \textit{Fos} in Neurons} --- The activation of these immediate early genes serves as a proxy signature for severe neuronal depolarization, supporting the hypothesis that these cells are undergoing chronic seizure-like hyperexcitability.
\end{itemize}

---

\section{Hypothesis 2: The C420R Mutation Promotes Actomyosin-Driven Mechanical Compression}

In contrast to the synaptic wiring model, we hypothesize that the \textit{C420R} mutation promotes mechanical migration and physical invasion. Rather than plugging into neural circuits, \textit{C420R} tumor cells may utilize actomyosin contractile forces---similar to the molecular machinery used in muscle cells---to squeeze, crawl, and physically compress host brain tissue. The surrounding neurons are hypothesized to undergo structural and cytoskeletal remodeling to survive this physical pressure, without suffering the metabolic collapse observed in the \textit{H1047R} cohort.

\subsection*{Key Data Supporting the Actomyosin Motility Hypothesis (Malignant Cells vs. Control):}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Striated Muscle Contraction} ($+2.81$ NES) \& \textbf{Skeletal Muscle Contraction} ($+2.59$ NES) --- Suggests the activation of contractile fibers for migration.
    \item \textbf{Muscle Contraction} ($+2.43$ NES) \& \textbf{Myofibril Assembly} ($+2.38$ NES) --- Indicates the assembly of crawling machinery.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Key Data Supporting the Neuronal Compression \& Remodeling Hypothesis (Neurons - C420R vs. Control):}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Muscle Contraction} ($+2.82$ NES) \& \textbf{Myofibril Assembly} ($+2.74$ NES) --- Suggests host neurons are actively changing shape to adapt to physical pressure.
    \item \textbf{Striated Muscle Contraction} ($+2.71$ NES) --- Indicates mechanical cytoskeletal remodeling.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Single-Cell Machine Learning \& SHAP Driver Gene Hypotheses:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{\textit{Tmsb4x} (Thymosin Beta-4) in Tumor Cells} --- The identification of this actin-sequestering protein supports the hypothesis that \textit{C420R} tumor cells heavily prioritize active actin filament construction to drive migration.
    \item \textbf{\textit{Csrp1} in Neurons} --- The upregulation of this cytoskeletal organizer in host neurons supports the hypothesis that they are undergoing structural adaptations to survive physical compression from the invading tumor mass.
\end{itemize}
